The best available answer comes from the largest study of real usage: OpenAI and academic researchers analyzed **1.5 million ChatGPT conversations** (privacy-protected) to see what people do with it. The picture is surprisingly down-to-earth:

::viz usage-chart::

**Three everyday jobs dominate.** About ==8 in 10 conversations== are practical guidance (how-tos, tutoring, ideas), looking things up, or help with writing. The futuristic stuff you see in headlines is a rounding error next to "help me write this email."

**Most use is personal, not professional.** Roughly ==70% of use is outside work== — planning trips, understanding health letters, fixing recipes, helping kids with homework. At work, writing help is the biggest single use by far.

**The surprise: coding is tiny.** Programming makes up only around 4% of conversations. AI is famous as a coder's tool, but by volume it's overwhelmingly a *words* tool — reading, writing, explaining.

**What this means for you:** you're not behind, and you don't need a use case worthy of a keynote. If you've ever asked it to reword an email or explain a confusing letter, you're already using AI the way most of the world does. Start with the boring stuff — that's where it's genuinely great.
