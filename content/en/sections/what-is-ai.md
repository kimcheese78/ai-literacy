**AI is software that learned from examples instead of being programmed with rules.** Show a computer millions of photos labeled "cat," and it figures out cat-ness on its own. That's the core trick behind everything you're hearing about.

**The AI everyone is talking about right now is the chatbot kind** — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini. These are powered by *large language models*: programs that have read a huge portion of the internet and learned one skill extremely well — predicting which words come next.

**Here's the one analogy to keep:** a chatbot is **autocomplete raised to a superpower**. Your phone suggests the next word; these models predict the next word so well, over such vast reading, that the result can draft emails, summarize contracts, explain physics, and write working code.

That single analogy also explains the two things beginners most need to know:

- **Why it's so useful:** it has "read" more than any human ever could, and it's endlessly patient. Great for drafting, summarizing, explaining, translating, and brainstorming.
- **Why you can't fully trust it:** it predicts *plausible* words, not *verified* facts. Sometimes it confidently makes things up — that's called a **hallucination** (defined below). Use it as a brilliant, fast, occasionally-wrong assistant — not as an encyclopedia.

**And what it is not:** it isn't conscious, it doesn't have goals or feelings, and it isn't a search engine (though many chatbots can now search the web when asked).
