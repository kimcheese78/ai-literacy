title: What should I actually do with this?

No course, no subscription, no technical skill. Four small moves, in order, over a week or so:

1. **Have one real conversation.** Pick any free chatbot — [Claude](https://claude.ai), [ChatGPT](https://chatgpt.com), or [Gemini](https://gemini.google.com) — and ask it about something *you* know deeply: your job, your hobby, your hometown. You'll see its strengths and flaws faster than any article can explain them.
2. **Catch it being wrong — on purpose.** Ask detailed questions about a niche topic you know well, and hunt for small errors. ==Finding your first mistake is the moment AI stops being magic and starts being a tool.==
3. **Use it for one real task this week.** Not a demo — something you actually need done. Rewrite an awkward email. Turn messy notes into a plan. Ask it to explain a confusing contract clause "like I'm not a lawyer."
4. **Learn the one prompting habit that matters: give context.** Compare "write a thank-you note" with "write a short, warm thank-you note to my daughter's teacher, who stayed late all term to run the science club." Same tool, wildly better result. ==Who it's for, what it's for, what tone — that's 80% of "prompt engineering."==

**A note on safety while you experiment:** don't paste in passwords, ID numbers, or anything confidential — treat a chatbot like a very smart stranger. And double-check anything factual before you act on it or repeat it.
