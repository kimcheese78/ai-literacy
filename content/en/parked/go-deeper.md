If this page did its job, you don't *need* anything else to start using AI well. But if you're curious, these are the free resources worth your time — chosen because they're beginner-friendly, trustworthy, and not trying to sell you anything.

- **[Elements of AI](https://www.elementsofai.com/)** — a gentle, famous free course from the University of Helsinki. No math, no code. The best structured next step for absolute beginners.
- **[AI for Everyone (DeepLearning.AI)](https://www.deeplearning.ai/courses/ai-for-everyone/)** — Andrew Ng's non-technical course; free to audit on Coursera. Great if you want to understand AI's impact on work and business.
- **[Intro to Large Language Models — Andrej Karpathy (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zjkBMFhNj_g)** — a legendary one-hour talk by a leading AI researcher. The single best "how does this actually work" explainer in plain language.
- **[How I Use LLMs — Andrej Karpathy (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EWvNQjAaOHw)** — the practical follow-up: watching an expert actually use chatbots day to day.
- **[Khan Academy: AI for Education](https://www.khanacademy.org/college-careers-more/ai-for-education)** — short, free lessons, especially good for students and teachers.

A fair warning before you wander beyond this list: most AI content online is trying to sell you a course, a newsletter, or a "secret prompt." The resources above are the exceptions. When in doubt, apply this page's own test — is it dated, is it sourced, and is a named person accountable for it?
